# Al Brooks Price Action Trading — Methodology Reference

## Market Cycle Classification

Markets exist in one of three states at all times:

**Trend**: Price moves directionally with follow-through bars. Strong trends have consecutive bars with bodies > 60% of range, minimal overlap between bars, and closes near extremes. The Always-In direction is clear.

**Channel**: A weaker form of trend with more overlap, deeper pullbacks, and mixed bar types. Still tradeable in the trend direction but requires smaller size and tighter stops.

**Trading Range**: Price oscillates between support and resistance. Bars overlap significantly, bodies are small relative to range, and direction alternates. Most breakout attempts fail (~80%). The correct strategy is to fade extremes with limit orders.

## Climax Detection

A **buy climax** occurs when 3 or more consecutive strong bull trend bars appear, often with expanding ranges. This represents unsustainable buying pressure — typically retail FOMO and algorithmic momentum chasing. Institutional traders begin taking profits during or immediately after the climax.

A **sell climax** is the mirror: 3+ consecutive strong bear bars with expanding ranges.

After any climax, expect a **TBTL correction**: approximately Ten Bars and Two Legs of counter-trend movement. The correction typically retraces 40-60% of the climax move.

## Always-In Direction

If forced to hold a position overnight, which side would you choose? This is the Always-In direction. It is determined by:
- The most recent strong trend move
- Whether the last breakout had follow-through
- Bar 1 of the session (strong trend bar = high probability of continuation)

## Order Flow Dynamics

**Institutional behavior**: Large players accumulate positions using limit orders to avoid market impact. They buy during pullbacks in trends and sell during rallies. They fade false breakouts at range boundaries because they know 80% of breakouts fail.

**Retail behavior**: Small traders use market orders and chase momentum. They buy after 3+ bull bars (FOMO), place stops at obvious round numbers and recent swing points, and panic exit on 2+ bars against their position. They exhibit disposition effect (cut winners, hold losers).

**The liquidity transfer**: When retail FOMO-buys a climax with market orders, institutional traders are the ones filling those orders with their limit sell orders. Retail provides the exit liquidity that institutions need. Then, when the TBTL correction triggers retail stop-losses, institutions buy those stops to accumulate new positions.

## Key Patterns

**High-2**: In a bull trend, the second pullback to a lower low is a buy setup. The pullback tests whether buyers are still in control.

**Low-2**: In a bear trend, the second rally to a higher high is a sell setup.

**False Breakout**: Price breaks above range resistance (or below support) but closes back inside the range. This traps breakout traders and provides a high-probability fade entry.

**Measured Move**: The expected correction after a trend move equals the distance of the move. If price rallied 10 points before the climax, expect approximately 10 points of correction.

**Gap Open**: When RTH opens with a gap against the overnight trend, there is approximately 50% probability of continuation vs reversal. The first 6-12 bars involve aggressive directional testing by institutional traders.
